Samuel (Sam) T. Woodrich

Sam is an ecologist based in the Willamette Valley of Oregon. He is an expert researching trophic cascades and dynamics, native and non-native species interactions, and avian ecology. Beyond his research, he has experience in botany, conservation biology, population dynamics, occupancy estimation and modeling, restoration ecology, and geospatial analysis.

Sam has led and contributed to several field studies including carbon stock and biodiversity estimates in Tenmile Creek, Oregon, native ungulate impacts on patch dynamics of the Cummins Ridge Wilderness Area, riparian vegetation response to wolf reintroduction in Yellowstone National Park, and avian presence estimates in the High Cascades region, to name a few. Additionally, Sam is a scientific writer and has been published in Restoration Ecology, Food Webs, and BioScience.

In his spare time, Sam is usually bird watching, botanizing, hiking, or engaged in some other outdoor activity.
